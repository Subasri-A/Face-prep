Nature refers to the phenomena of the physical world collectively, including plants, animals, the landscape, and other features and products of the earth, as opposed to human creations. It encompasses the entire universe and everything that exists within it, from the smallest particles to the vast expanses of space.

Animals are multicellular, eukaryotic organisms belonging to the biological kingdom Animalia. They are characterized by their ability to move, consume organic material, breathe oxygen, and reproduce sexually. Most animals are motile, meaning they can move independently and spontaneously at some point in their life cycle.

Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves. They are characterized by feathers, a beak without teeth, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeletal structure.
